ABOUT ME

I got into eLearning design and web development by accident. If it wasn’t for lockdown and some coincidences, I might still be under the impression that programming is mystical and unreachable, something only people with special powers understand.

In 2021, I spent a lot of my time learning to code: from HTML, CSS and Vanilla JavaScript and frontend frameworks like Bootstrap and Vue JS, to backend languages such as PHP and MySQL. By the end of it, I built a full-stack eCommerce site – something I may not have thought possible a year earlier.

Since mid-2020, I’ve also been assisting an eLearning designer on a freelance basis. I learned how to work with Moodle, creating layouts and populating pages with learning content, designing infographics in Adobe Illustrator and Articulate Storyline, and applying my newly acquired coding skills to create learning interactions.

Working with pre-coded H5P interactions in Moodle is what inspired me to learn how to code. I wanted to apply more creativity and control and – one day – be able to build fully customised learning experiences. Discovering eLearning experience design has opened up a massive scope for opportunity and satisfaction – it combines my diverse interests into a streamlined process with a goal: to bring concepts closer to people using technology and creativity.

I have a background in journalism, studying Film and Media Production at UCT and writing articles for publications such as GroundUp or Africa is a Country. Pre-Covid, I worked as an events photographer and created a mini documentary about the tango community in Cape Town.

Things I love: the ocean, learning to play musical instruments (violin, Irish bouzouki, bodhrán and bones), and dancing tango.

I'm currently studying User Centred Design at Red & Yellow.
