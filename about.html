ABOUT ME

If it wasn’t for lockdown and some lucky coincidences, I might still be under the impression that web development and programming is something only people with special powers understand.

But as things turned out, I spent a lot of 2021 learning to code: from HTML, CSS and Vanilla JavaScript and frontend frameworks like Bootstrap and Vue JS, to backend languages such as PHP and MySQL. By the end of it, I built a full-stack e-commerce site – something I may not have thought possible a year earlier.

My web development journey was inspired by an introduction to e-learning design and interactivity. In 2020 in the midst of lockdown, I started assisting an e-learning developer build courses. Together we turned Word documents submitted by SMEs into interactive learning experiences through creating seamless layouts, designing clear-cut infographics, as well as writing and building engaging learner activities. The idea of using design to enhance knowledge acquisition was exciting to me. I wanted to learn more about how technology could improve people's lives so I did a full-stack web development course followed by an Advanced Diploma in User-Centred Design at Red & Yellow Creative School of Business in Cape Town.

During this time, I was able to apply my interests in mental health, music and technology-enhanced learning to a year-long independent study project: a singing platform for individuals with social anxiety. This gave me a sense of purpose, and sparked more ideas and excitement about the direction I was taking. Designing for mental health, and using technology to empower people is what I’m passionate about.

One of the most important things I have come to understand about learning a new skill is that anything is possible at any time, but it’s not always an easy path. Finding solutions to problems requires patience, but is highly satisfying – if one remains calm. I was now able to see a project through, start to finish: from conducting UX research, to creating learning content and interactivity, to designing a prototype – and eventually using my coding skills to bring a concept to life.

I have a background in journalism, studying Film and Media Production at The University of Cape Town and writing articles for publications such as GroundUp or Africa is a Country. Pre-Covid, I worked as an events photographer and created a mini documentary about the tango community in Cape Town.

Things I love: the ocean, learning to play musical instruments (violin, Irish bouzouki, bodhrán and bones), and dancing tango.
