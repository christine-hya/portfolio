E-LEARNING DESIGN & DEVELOPMENT

From 2020 to 2022, I assisted a freelance e-learning designer create online courses for a variety of topics, from digital transformation, AI and business management, to community education and volunteering, to remote orientation modules for university students. Becoming familiar with concepts such as chunking, signalling and micro-learning, I created interactive learning experiences using Moodle, Bootstrap, H5P, and Articulate Storyline. I also designed infographics and course notes in Adobe Illustrator and InDesign, wrote learning content, such as quizzes and scenarios, and coded some simple interactive elements.

In 2022, as part of a course in User-Centred Design, I completed a three-week internship in the instructional design department at Red & Yellow Creative School of Business. This strengthened my ability to write learning content that is easy to digest and the appropriate length for a set amount of notional hours, and how to link learning interactivity to instructional design concepts such as Merrill's Principles.

UX/UI DESIGN

I have completed an Advanced Diploma in User-Centred Design at Red & Yellow Creative School of Business. From conceptualising an idea to high-fidelity prototyping, I learned about UX research methods, ideation tools, information architecture and visual design techniques. There was a strong focus on practical application, including multiple live briefs from real-world clients, an independent study and a three-week internship. Through facing real design challenges, I was able to practise my problem-solving skills and understand how important it is to base design choices on rigorous user research.

Beyond conducting multiple usability tests on existing websites, as well as extensive user research, I also improved my visual design skills. A section of the course was dedicated to how the Gestalt Principles relate to usability, and we applied this knowledge to infographic design, as well as the re-design of a parking ticket machine, a product page for a sports retailer, a donation process for an education NGO's website and a parent-app-linked smartwatch app for kids. To ensure designs were modular, it was essential to grasp the benefits of design systems, and the usage of styles and components in Figma.

Read my case study on designing an app to help parents monitor their children's health here.
